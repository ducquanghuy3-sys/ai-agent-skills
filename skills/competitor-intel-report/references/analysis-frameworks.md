# Competitive Analysis Frameworks

## 1. Porter's Five Forces (Industry Attractiveness)

Apply to the competitor's industry:

| Force | Assessment | Impact |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **Threat of New Entrants** | How easy to enter? Capital requirements, regulation, network effects, brand moats | High/Med/Low |
| **Bargaining Power of Suppliers** | Key dependencies (cloud, APIs, content). Switching costs. | High/Med/Low |
| **Bargaining Power of Buyers** | Switching costs for customers. Alternatives available. Price sensitivity. | High/Med/Low |
| **Threat of Substitutes** | Can the problem be solved differently? Adjacent products eating share? | High/Med/Low |
| **Competitive Rivalry** | Number of competitors, differentiation level, market growth rate, exit barriers | Intense/Moderate/Low |

**Industry Attractiveness Score**: Sum of forces вҶ’ Very Attractive / Attractive / Neutral / Unattractive

## 2. Jobs-to-be-Done Framework

Analyze what "job" the competitor's product is hired to do:

```
FUNCTIONAL JOB:
"When I [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [desired outcome]."

EMOTIONAL JOB:
"I want to feel [emotion] when I [context]."

SOCIAL JOB:
"I want to be seen as [perception] by [audience]."
```

**Application**: Identify jobs the competitor serves poorly вҶ’ opportunity for your product.

**Switching forces analysis:**
- Push (away from current): [Pain points with competitor]
- Pull (toward new): [Attractions of your solution]
- Inertia (stay): [Switching costs, habits, data lock-in]
- Anxiety (about new): [Risks of switching]

Win when: Push + Pull > Inertia + Anxiety

## 4. Moat Analysis (Competitive Advantage)

Assess each competitor's moat:

| Moat Type | Score | Evidence |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| **Network Effects** | 0-5 | More users вҶ’ more value for each user? |
| **Switching Costs** | 0-5 | Data lock-in, workflow dependency, training investment |
| **Brand** | 0-5 | Mind share, trust, willingness to pay premium |
| **Scale Economies** | 0-5 | Unit costs decrease with growth? |
| **Distribution** | 0-5 | Exclusive channels, partnerships, shelf space |
| **IP / Technology** | 0-5 | Patents, proprietary tech, data assets |
| **Regulatory** | 0-5 | Licenses, compliance, government relationships |

Total Moat Score: /35
- 25+: Very strong moat (hard to compete directly)
- 15-24: Moderate moat (can be challenged with right strategy)
- <15: Weak moat (vulnerable to new entrants)

## 5. Pricing Strategy Framework

Analyze competitor pricing through these lenses:

**Value Metric**: What do they charge for? (Per seat / per usage / per feature / flat rate)

**Pricing Model**: 
- Freemium (free tier вҶ’ paid conversion)
- Free trial (time-limited full access)
- Usage-based (pay for what you use)
- Flat-rate (simple, predictable)
- Hybrid (combination)

**Price Positioning**:
- Premium (>20% above market average)
- Market rate (Вұ20% of average)
- Penetration (>20% below average)
- Predatory (unsustainably low, funded by VC)

**Monetization Gaps**: Features/value they give away free that could be monetized, or overprice that creates opportunity.

## 6. GTM Motion Classification

| Motion | Description | Signals |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Product-Led Growth (PLG)** | Product sells itself, free tier, viral loops | Sign-up without sales, in-product sharing |
| **Sales-Led Growth (SLG)** | Enterprise sales team, demos, contracts | "Contact sales", annual contracts, SDR hiring |
| **Community-Led Growth (CLG)** | Community drives adoption and advocacy | Active Discord/forum, user conferences, champions |
| **Content-Led Growth** | SEO and content drives acquisition | High blog volume, keyword targeting, organic traffic |
| **Partnership-Led** | Integrations and partners drive distribution | Large marketplace, partner programs, API ecosystem |

Most companies use 1-2 primary motions. Identify which and assess their effectiveness.
